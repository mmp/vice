
The N90 TRACON is unusually complex, including having multiple airports
with high traffic volume (JFK and LGA) that are only ten miles apart.
Two more high-volume airports--EWR and TEB--are also nearby, a bit further to the West; these were
to be part of N90 until 2025, when a separate TRACON for EWR was established
and the associated controllers moved to the PHL TRACON building.
An single extended Bravo airspace volume covers JFK, LGA, and EWR.

Further complicating things, there is often a significant amount of GA
traffic when the weather is good, including many aircraft along the Hudson
River to the West of Manhattan. (Some are in Bravo airspace and some are outside of the
Bravo, flying in the exclusion zone, under 1,400'.)

Of the other controlled airports in the TRACON, ISP is class Charlie,
and TEB, HPN, MMU, DXR, FRG, OXC, HVN, CDW, BDR, and FOK are all class Delta.

# Departure Procedures

Low-altitude departures from N90 airports leaving the TRACON are handed off to adjacent TRACONs
(e.g. ZBW BDL to the Northeast and WRI to the Southwest) if they are not climbing to enroute
altitudes.  Otherwise they may go to NY Center, Boston Center, or to Liberty departure, an N90-wide
(+EWR and TEB) departure position that merges departure flows from all area airports, providing
necessary spacing in the flows of aircraft that they hand off to center.

## LGA Final Vectors

The flow for final vectors is highly-dependent on the configuration: in particular, arrivals are routed to the opposite side of the field from departures, so that departures may climb without interfering with arrivals.

```configurations lga-final
L4D13: Land 4 / Depart 13
L4D31: Land 4 / Depart 31
L31X: Land RNAV X / Park Visual 31
L31Z: Land RNAV Z / LOC 31
LI13: Land ILS 13
L22D13: Land 22 / Depart 13
L22D31: Land 22 / Depart 31
```

::: if lga-final:L4D13,lga-final:L4D31
In this configuration, in general, there is not much room to vector the EMPYR flow, other than
slightly adjusting spacing through s-turns and speed adjustments.  For the most part, the task is to
vector VADDR1 arrivals to fly Southwest along the Special Use Line (on a 220 heading, though wind
may require adjustments) and then to turn them to base and then to intercept gaps in the EMPYR
flow.

Per discussion of the SUL above, aircraft should be at or above 4,000' along the SUL and should
only be descended further after given a base turn and are 3nm from the SUL.

::: endif lga-final:L4D13,lga-final:L4D31

::: if lga-final:L31X
Note that HAARP should hand off arrivals on a ~220 heading, offset a few miles Southeast of the field
in order to keep clear of runway 22 departures.
Once past LGA, aircraft should be vectored to the West to fly downwind along the SUL at 4,000'
before being merged with the EMPYR flow and turned to final.
::: endif lga-final:L31X

::: if lga-final:L31Z
Note that additional airspace so the Southeast of LGA is delegated from JFK to LGA in this configuration,
allowing the final controller space to merge the EMPYR and VADDR1 flows to the East of the field.
There is limited space to vector VADDR1 arrivals for spacing; merging by turning EMPYR aircraft to join
the VADDR1 flow is generally best.
::: endif lga-final:L31Z

::: if lga-final:L22D13
VADDR1 arrivals are generally given "direct YOMAN, join the localizer".
The EMPYR flow flies a right downwind to remain clear of Runway 13 departures.
It should be merged into the VADDR1 arrival flow; there is a few miles of airspace to the Northwest of final that can be used if needed.
::: endif lga-final:L22D13

::: if lga-final:L22D31
VADDR1 arrivals are generally given "direct YOMAN, join the localizer".

## JFK Area

There are a number of primary arrival flows into the JFK area:
- JFK/FRG: the PUCKY1, coming from the Northwest, handed off by NY Center.
- JFK/FRG: the PARCH4 from the Northeast, coming from Boston Center.
- JFK: the CAMRN5/OWENZ flow from the South. 
- FRG: the SARDI1 arrival from the South

FRG may also have arrivals via intra-TRACON routes from adjoining facilities.

These are handled differently depending on which runways are in use for arrivals:

```configurations jfk-land
4: Land 4R (4L)
13: Land 13L (13R)
22: Land 22L (22R)
31: Land 31R (31L)
```

Aircraft on the PUCKY1 are handed off at FL190, 250kts. They should be given a descent to 16000' upon entering the TRACON's airspace, indicated by the
video map boundary.

::: if jfk-land:4,jfk-land:13
Landing 4s or 13s, arrivals are on a 136 heading after VADDR. Once they are to the East of JFK, there is a pre-arranged coordination
procedure with JFK Departure (2J): after an accepted point-out to 2J, aircraft may be put on a 180 heading and given a descent to 8000'
as they fly through departure's airspace.
They are then handed off to CAMRN;
once they exit departure's airspace, they may be given a descent to 4000' and merged into the other arrival flows.
::: endif jfk-land:4,jfk-land:13

::: if jfk-land:22,jfk-land:31
Landing 22s or 31s, arrivals are put on roughly a 180 heading departing PUCKY with a series of additional descents when they are possible, given airspace constraints. In either case, they end up on a 040(ish) heading roughly 15 miles away from the airport before they are handed off to CAMRN to be merged into the other arrival flows.
::: endif jfk-land:22,jfk-land:31
